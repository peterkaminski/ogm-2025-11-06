Jerry Michalski

Key Insights: Non-Local Consciousness, GABA, and Psilocybin

Jerry's check-in wove together three interconnected themes from recent reading and a transformative personal experience.

Mr. Rogers T-Shirt Story

Small thing: Wearing a "Beautiful Day in this Neighborhood" t-shirt from Pittsburgh's Mr. Rogers Museum

Significance: April (Jerry's partner) visited after giving a talk. Mr. Rogers played a crucial role in her childhood, telling her "she was okay just the way she is" during a difficult relationship with her mother.

Artifact: Brought back a "Kindness Zone" road sign-style decoration for their wall.

Non-Local Consciousness

Source: Reading Dan Brown's latest novel "The Secret of Secrets"

Plot Point: Non-local consciousness - the idea that consciousness isn't generated by neurons but is "background radiation of the universe" that we "tune into like a radio station"

Connection to Previous Thinking:

  • Sunil Malhotra suggested this concept ~1.5 years ago on an OGM call
  • Jerry realized he'd already encountered this from different research
  • "Gets you into woo-woo noetic science territory, which I'm beginning to respect a lot more"

Parallels with Other Work:

  • The Da Vinci Code → Divine Feminine marginalization thesis
  • Leonard Shlain's "The Alphabet vs. the Goddess" (same thesis, academic perspective)
  • Jerry's own decades of work on "consumer" as concept
  • All clicked together with yin/yang thinking

GABA (Gamma-Aminobutyric Acid)

From Dan Brown's Book: GABA is the primary inhibitor in our neural system.

Function:

  • High levels of GABA keep us from being overwhelmed by stimuli
  • Low GABA opens up perception dramatically
  • Epilepsy mechanism: precipitous drop in GABA → brain overwhelmed → seizure
  • Epileptics experience moments of "bliss" and "connection with the infinite" after grand mal seizures

Connection to Consciousness Theory: Hallucinogens may not generate hallucinations but rather reduce GABA levels, lowering filters so you can "perceive what's always happening around you"

Psilocybin Journey

Timing: Wednesday afternoon before the call (his FIRST trip ever - "I've never done drugs")

Setting:

  • Friend in neighborhood who is "completely trustworthy"
  • Friend grew the mushrooms himself, tested them
  • Incredibly safe and lovely experience

Experience:

  • "Phenomenal" and "really transformative"
  • Felt connected to "mycelial networks of the world"
  • Felt like "everything all at once-ish"
  • Gained new understanding of special effects in movies
  • "Tried... to leave myself little doors or candles or gateways to step back into the experience" without repeating
  • Still processing

Impact: "I feel like I'm transformed a bit, I don't know how."

Later in Call

  • One person at a time, no conversation during check-ins
  • Pick something "OGM-y" that "exemplifies what you've been up to"
  • Embrace silence and quiet space
  • Switch to conversation mode after everyone checks in
