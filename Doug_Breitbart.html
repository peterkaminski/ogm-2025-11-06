Doug Breitbart

Key Insight: Tab Groups as Tab Emancipation

Doug shared a "life-changing" personal discovery of browser tab groups that freed him from overwhelming tab overload, connected to his broader philosophy about human potential.

The Discovery

Context: At 69 years old, as an ADD person, "my executive function is finally fully matured"

Result: "Huge expansion of capacity capability to grapple with things and play with things, and do things, and use things" that were "opaque and impenetrable and unusable before"

The Tab Problem

Before:

  • 4-5 browser windows across 3 monitors
  • 45-50 tabs per window
  • Total: ~250 tabs
  • Tabs so small they couldn't be differentiated
  • "Emotionally, they're there. They exist."
  • Approaching computer in morning: "overwhelming crap. Like, what do I do now?"

Discovery Process:

  • Knew the capability had to exist
  • Saw something flash
  • Noticed "Tab Groups" in a menu
  • "Okay, I think this is my path to emancipation, to tab emancipation"

Implementation:

  • Because of "newly vested executive function," able to learn it quickly
  • Sorted and bucketed 250 tabs into groups
  • Closed the groups
  • Closed browser windows
  • Freed up computer resources
  • "My poor little laptop that could" - was asking "mini-computer demands" of it
  • Now: "only have the one or two tab groups up that were relevant in that moment"

Impact: "This is life-changing for someone like me"

The Bigger Picture: Zooming Way Out

Core Belief: "Every human being has value to add and contribute"

Three Key Inquiries:

  1. Foundational: Every human has value to contribute (if you buy this...)

  2. Operational: "What do each one of those individuals need in order to be enabled to contribute?"

  3. Systemic: "How do we meet everyone's needs?"

Everything Else: "If it is not germane to those inquiries is substantially irrelevant"

Personal Impact

  • "Flood tide of capacity"
  • Organizing
  • Moving into action
  • "Unleashing the hounds in all sorts of ways"
