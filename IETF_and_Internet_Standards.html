IETF and Internet Standards

Note: Discussions mentioned by Mike Nelson as part of his overstimulation during the OGM 2025-11-06 call.

What is IETF?

Internet Engineering Task Force - organization that develops and promotes voluntary internet standards

Meeting Week of Call

IETF was meeting during the week of the OGM call, with "lively debates" about:

1. New Authentication System

For digital transactions - details shared in chat

Standard: draft-sbriz-identity-trust-system-04

  • Digital identity authentication system
  • Based on symmetric exchange
  • Doesn't require federation of authentication domains
  • Link: https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-sbriz-identity-trust-system/04/

2. Content Scraping Prevention

Tool to allow websites to prevent content scraping

Working Group: AI Preferences (aipref)

  • Standardize how to express preferences
  • About content collection for AI
  • About processing for AI model development/deployment
  • Link: https://datatracker.ietf.org/wg/aipref/about/

The Dilemma

Mike's framing: "How do you stop a bad person from abusing the openness of the internet while allowing a researcher to span 100,000 websites and search through terabytes of data?"

Examples:

  • Bots scraping for social security numbers (bad)
  • Researchers analyzing trends across web (good)
  • McDonald's preventing Burger King from seeing prices (?)
  • Putting "huge demand" on non-profit websites

Chinese Digital Identity

Separate concern: China pushing forward digital identity system with:

  • Surveillance built in
  • Trying to get multinationals to use it
  • Can't go anonymously between services

Mike's Point

These crucial technical decisions happening "below the radar" while everyone focuses on "whether AI will destroy world civilization"
